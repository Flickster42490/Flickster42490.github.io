Makersquare week 1

2015-08-02

I made it to SF exactly one week ago with a single suitcase full of clothes and my laptop, ready to kick off my journey into the world of programmer development at Makersquare. Between then and now, I’ve had exactly one free day–today–to set up my blog and actually write about what I’ve been up to. There’s around 20 students in my July 2015 cohort of varying ages, but the majority are in their late twenties to early thirties. Backgrounds range from fresh out of college graduates with biomedical degrees to those with almost a decade of work experience in finance, tech, or business.

On day 1, there were the usual lectures about procedures and expectations, but before the first hour was up, we were instructed to find our pairs for the Underscore and Recursion pair programming sprints and start reconstructing around 20 or so functions in the Underscore library. This was just the beginning of a crazy, exhausting, and hectic week. By the end of the week, there were over 25 lectures on all things Makersquare and sprints for the week, countless hours pair coding, and discussions with cohortmates on the different ways hoisting works in that inner function or whatever problems we seemed to encounter each hour. We finished sprints on Underscore library, recursion, basic and more advanced data structures (stack, linkedlists, graphs, hash tables, binary trees, etc).

I felt as if I was constantly wavering between three different states: astonished (over how I could have possibly learned so much more in a week than I did previously with 4 months of self-study), euphoria (when my pair and I would finally get our implementation of a hash table to spit out the right values), and sleep deprivation. Let me explain the last state. Makersquare officially runs Monday through Saturday from 9am-8pm. Unofficially, I’ve been coming in around 8am to preview the next sprints and leaving sometime between 9-11pm either finish implementing our code for the sprint with my pair or refactoring previously finished ones. There are also occasional after hours presentations by the fellows on topics ranging from setting up your dev environment to writing reusable code.

In any case, week 1 down! and 12 more to go!
